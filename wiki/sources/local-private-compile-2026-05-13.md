# Local Private Compile 2026-05-13

This source record represents a local-only compile pass used to decide what can be safely rewritten into the public wiki. The underlying material stayed outside the public graph. This page records only the public interpretation boundary.

## What Was Learned

- The public archive should be a compiled knowledge product, not a dump of local files.
- The strongest public themes are knowledge-base architecture, public data boundaries, agent skill governance, and repeatable promotion workflow.
- Project and capability claims should be rewritten into reusable lessons before they appear in `wiki/`.
- Items that depend on private conversations, local setup details, account state, or unrevised personal records remain out of public pages.

## Why It Matters

Karpathy's LLM Wiki pattern depends on the wiki becoming the durable artifact. For a personal archive, that artifact must also be safe to publish. This means the public layer needs source notes, distilled concepts, and synthesis pages that explain the system without leaking the private evidence behind it.
